Understanding Civil Law in India

Civil law deals with non-criminal disputes and includes various legal areas such as property disputes, contract issues, family law, and torts. The Indian legal system, based on common law principles, governs these matters through statutes, regulations, and judicial precedents. Key legislations and codes relevant to civil law in India include:

  • Indian Contract Act, 1872: Governs contracts and agreements, including aspects related to the formation, performance, and enforcement of contracts.
  • Transfer of Property Act, 1882: Regulates the transfer of property and deals with issues related to property transactions, ownership, and rights.
  • Family Law Acts: Includes various statutes such as the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, the Special Marriage Act, 1954, and the Divorce Act, 1869, which govern family-related matters, including marriage, divorce, and child custody.
  • Civil Procedure Code (CPC), 1908: Outlines the procedures for civil litigation, including the filing of suits, evidence presentation, and appeal processes.
  • Consumer Protection Act, 2019: Provides protection to consumers against unfair trade practices, defective goods, and deficient services.
