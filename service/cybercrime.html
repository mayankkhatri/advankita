Understanding Cyber Crime in India

Cyber crime encompasses a wide range of illegal activities conducted through digital means, including the internet, computer systems, and mobile devices. In India, cyber crimes are addressed under various laws and regulations, and the legal framework is evolving to keep pace with technological advancements. Key legislations and regulations related to cyber crime in India include:

  • Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act): This is the primary legislation governing cyber crime in India. It defines various offenses and penalties related to cyber activities, including hacking, identity theft, data theft, and cyber fraud.
  • Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860: Certain sections of the IPC also apply to cyber crimes, such as those related to criminal breach of trust, forgery, and defamation when committed through digital means.
  • The IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021: These rules provide guidelines for intermediaries and digital media platforms, ensuring compliance with legal standards and addressing grievances related to content and cyber crimes.
  • Cyber Crime Investigation and Prosecution Guidelines: Issued by law enforcement agencies, these guidelines help in the investigation and prosecution of cyber crime cases, providing a structured approach for handling such cases.
