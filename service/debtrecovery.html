Debt Recovery Tribunal

The **Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT)** is a specialized legal forum in India that adjudicates matters related to the recovery of debts due to banks and financial institutions. Established under the **Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 (RDDBFI Act)**, the DRT is empowered to handle cases involving a large amount of debt, typically over ₹20 lakhs.

Advocate Ankita Goswami and her team provide comprehensive legal services in matters involving the DRT, representing both creditors (banks and financial institutions) and debtors. Understanding the complexities involved in these cases, she offers strategic advice and practical solutions to expedite the recovery process while adhering to the legal framework.

What is the Debt Recovery Tribunal?

The DRT was created to fast-track the process of debt recovery, which was previously slow and inefficient through traditional civil courts. The tribunal has the authority to hear and decide applications filed by banks or financial institutions to recover outstanding debts. It can also issue orders to attach properties, conduct inquiries, and determine liability quickly and efficiently.

Unlike traditional courts, the DRT has specialized expertise in debt recovery matters, making it an ideal forum for resolving these cases effectively. It also offers an appeal mechanism through the **Debt Recovery Appellate Tribunal (DRAT)** for parties seeking further redress.

Steps in a Debt Recovery Tribunal Case

The typical procedure in a DRT case includes:

  1. Filing of an application by the creditor (bank or financial institution) in the DRT.
  2. Notice to the debtor for response or defense.
  3. Submission of evidence by both parties.
  4. Hearing of arguments by the tribunal.
  5. Issuance of the tribunal’s decision or order regarding the recovery of the debt.
  6. Option to appeal the decision in the **Debt Recovery Appellate Tribunal (DRAT)**.
